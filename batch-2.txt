#	Offence / Query	BNS Section
1	Murder by a group of 5+ on grounds of race, caste, or personal belief (Mob Lynching)	Section 103(2) – Punishment is death or life imprisonment with fine; a landmark new specific provision.
2	Snatching (new offence for chain/bag snatching)	Section 304 – Punishable with imprisonment up to 3 years and fine.
3	Organised Crime (first time in general penal law)	Section 111 – Covers organised criminal syndicates; punishable with rigorous imprisonment of 5 years to life, or death.
4	Terrorist Act (incorporated into general penal law)	Section 113 – Punishable with rigorous imprisonment of life or death in fatal cases.
5	Sexual intercourse by deceitful means / false promise of marriage	Section 69 – Punishable with imprisonment up to 10 years and fine.
6	Death by rash driving + fleeing the scene without reporting	Section 106(2) – Enhanced punishment of up to 10 years and fine; a new specific sub-section.
7	Unnatural Offences (IPC Section 377 equivalent)	Not retained in BNS – Consensual same-sex acts were decriminalised by the Supreme Court (Navtej Singh Johar, 2018); BNS omits this provision entirely.
8	Receiving / Concealing Stolen Property (consolidated)	Section 317 – All related provisions consolidated here; punishable with imprisonment up to 3 years and/or fine.
9	General Attempt to Commit Suicide by a distressed person	Effectively decriminalised – BNS omits the IPC Section 309 equivalent for ordinary citizens, aligning with the Mental Healthcare Act, 2017.
10	Attempt to commit suicide to compel/restrain a public servant	Section 226 – Punishable with simple imprisonment up to 1 year and/or fine.
11	Criminal Conspiracy	Section 61 – Punishment varies with the gravity of the conspired offence.
12	Adultery	Not present in BNS – Omitted entirely, as IPC Section 497 was struck down by the Supreme Court in Joseph Shine v. Union of India (2018).
13	General Exception: Act of a person of Unsound Mind	Section 22 – Mirrors the old IPC Section 84; act is not an offence if done in a state of unsoundness of mind.
14	Right of Private Defence extending to causing Death	Section 37 – Specifies seven circumstances where the right extends to voluntarily causing death.
15	Acts endangering Sovereignty, Unity & Integrity of India (replaced Sedition)	Section 152 – Broader and stricter than old Section 124A IPC; punishable with life imprisonment or up to 7 years and fine.
16	Criminal Intimidation	Section 351 – Punishable with imprisonment up to 2 years and/or fine; aggravated forms carry higher penalties.
17	House-trespass to commit an offence	Section 331 – Punishment depends on the nature of the offence intended; sub-sections prescribe varying terms.
18	Petty Organised Crime (vehicle theft, pickpocketing by a gang)	Section 112 – Punishable with imprisonment of 1 to 7 years and fine; a new and distinct provision.
19	Public Nuisance (definition)	Section 292 – Defines acts that cause common injury, danger, or annoyance to the public.
20	Gang Rape of a woman under 18 years of age	Section 70(2) – Punishable with rigorous imprisonment for the remainder of natural life or death.
