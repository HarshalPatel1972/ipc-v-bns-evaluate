#	Offence / Query	BNS Section
1	General Attempt to Commit an Offence punishable with imprisonment	Section 62 – Whoever attempts to commit an offence and fails is punishable with imprisonment up to half the longest term provided for that offence.
2	Joint Liability – Common Intention (act by several persons)	Section 3(5) – When a criminal act is done by several persons in furtherance of a common intention, each is liable as if it were done by them alone.
3	General Exception: Child Under Seven Years of Age	Section 20 – Nothing is an offence done by a child under 7 years of age; absolute immunity from criminal liability.
4	General Exception: Act Done by Accident or Misfortune	Section 24 – Nothing is an offence done by accident or misfortune while performing a lawful act in a lawful manner with due care.
5	Right of Private Defence (primary declaration)	Section 34 – Declares that nothing is an offence done in the exercise of the right of private defence of body or property.
6	Abetment of a Thing (definition)	Section 45 – Defines abetment as instigation, conspiracy, or intentional aiding to commit an act.
7	Waging War Against the Government of India	Section 147 – Punishable with death or life imprisonment, and fine; the gravest offence against the State.
8	Promoting Enmity Between Different Groups (religion, race, language, etc.)	Section 196 – Punishable with imprisonment up to 3 years and/or fine; 5 years if committed at a place of worship.
9	Deliberate & Malicious Acts Outraging Religious Feelings	Section 299 – Punishable with imprisonment up to 3 years and/or fine.
10	False Charge of an Offence with Intent to Injure	Section 250 – Punishable with imprisonment up to 7 years and fine, mirroring the gravity of the falsely charged offence.
11	Harbouring or Concealing an Offender to screen from legal punishment	Section 238 – Punishable with imprisonment varying with the gravity of the offence committed by the person harboured.
12	Voluntarily Causing a Woman with Child to Miscarry (Abortion)	Section 88 – Punishable with imprisonment up to 3 years and/or fine; up to 7 years if the woman is quick with child.
13	Kidnapping or Maiming a Minor for Begging	Section 141 – Punishable with rigorous imprisonment up to 10 years and fine; a child-protective provision.
14	Trafficking of Persons (definition and punishment)	Section 143 – Punishable with rigorous imprisonment of 7 to 10 years and fine; aggravated forms attract life imprisonment.
15	Unlawfully Compelling a Person to Labour Against Their Will	Section 144 – Punishable with imprisonment up to 1 year and/or fine, or both.
16	Habitually Dealing in Slaves	Section 142 – Punishable with imprisonment for life or rigorous imprisonment up to 10 years, and fine.
17	Mischief by Killing or Maiming an Animal	Section 325 – Punishable with imprisonment up to 5 years and/or fine for killing/maiming animals of prescribed value.
18	Deceitfully Inducing Belief of Lawful Marriage for Cohabitation	Section 84 – Punishable with imprisonment up to 10 years and fine; protects against fraudulent cohabitation.
19	Being a Thug (historical offence retained under BNS)	Section 310 – Whoever is a thug shall be punished with imprisonment for life and fine; retained from the original IPC equivalent.
20	Making/Publishing False Statements or Rumours Conducing to Public Mischief	Section 197 – Punishable with imprisonment up to 3 years and/or fine; up to 7 years during war or national emergency.
